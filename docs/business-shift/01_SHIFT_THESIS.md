# 01. Shift Thesis

**Prepared:** March 2026

## Core Shift

NorBot's current model is strongest when it replaces a contractor's weak or dated website with a branded ConversionOS tenant. A new signal has now appeared: some contractors like their current website, have recently invested in it, and do **not** want to throw it away. They still want the AI features that make ConversionOS valuable.

The shift under consideration is:

- keep the shared ConversionOS core
- keep white-labelling
- keep AI-native conversion features
- but let Dominate preserve more of the contractor's current website feel, structure, and public experience

The simplest expression is:

> "We keep the feel of the site you like, but move it onto our AI-native conversion platform."

## Triggering Customer Signal

### Facts

- The live MD Construction site has richer public-site structure than the current MD tenant in ConversionOS: utility bar, nested dropdowns, hero consultation form, FAQ/resources/blog depth, and theme-specific motion.
- MD Construction indicated a preference to keep the website they recently invested in while adding AI features.
- The current `products/demo` tenant architecture already supports the AI and admin side of that request, but not full public-site parity out of the box.
- The repo already includes a tenant-builder system that scrapes contractor sites, provisions tenants, and runs fidelity-style QA against the original site.

### Inference

This is not a one-off aesthetic complaint. It exposes a meaningful buying objection in the market:

- established contractors do not want to discard recent design investment
- they do not necessarily object to NorBot's platform
- they object to losing a website they already identify with

If NorBot can solve that objection cleanly, Dominate becomes materially more compelling.

## What Stays the Same About NorBot

This shift does **not** change NorBot's fundamental identity.

### Facts

- NorBot positions ConversionOS as white-label conversion infrastructure for contractors.
- The company posture is contractor-first, Ontario-first, ROI-driven, and not "AI hype."
- The platform core is already multi-tenant and data-isolated through `site_id`.
- The core differentiator remains the combination of visualization, structured intake, quote support, and faster lead handling.

### Inference

NorBot should still behave like a product company with implementation support, not a general custom web agency.

That means:

- shared platform remains the foundation
- custom shell work must stay bounded
- repeatable patterns should eventually be productized
- Black Label should remain reserved for broader bespoke automation

## What Changes in Dominate

Dominate becomes the tier where NorBot can say:

- we can preserve the feel of your current public site
- we can usually keep your current domain
- we can rebuild that experience on ConversionOS
- we can add native AI visualizer, chat, quote flow, admin, and analytics behind it

This is a stronger promise than "custom branding."

It is a different category of value:

- less visual disruption for the contractor
- lower psychological switching cost
- better leverage of existing brand trust
- clearer premium justification for the activation fee

## What Does Not Become Black Label

Black Label should not absorb ordinary contractor website-preservation work by default.

### Dominate should cover

- high-fidelity migration of a contractor website into a defined envelope
- same-domain cutover
- branded public experience plus full ConversionOS platform
- founder-led onboarding and selective integration

### Black Label should cover

- deeper business-process automation beyond the website/platform layer
- unusually complex technical estates
- multi-system workflows with heavy operational consulting
- cross-vertical or non-contractor engagements

## Why This Direction Is Attractive

### Facts

- The existing platform already supports the AI-native backend and admin value.
- The market increasingly understands AI reception, automation, and visualization.
- Contractors are familiar with website replacement pitches and may resist them after recent spend.

### Inference

This creates a wedge:

- "You do not need to throw away the site you just paid for."
- "You do not need to settle for a static brochure site."
- "You can keep your domain and familiarity while upgrading your conversion engine."

That message is likely easier to sell to established contractors than asking them to accept a full visual reset.

## Strategic Risk

The main risk is not feasibility. The main risk is **scope bleed**.

If NorBot cannot define what "preserve the feel of your site" means in a bounded way, this becomes agency work disguised as product.

That is why the rest of this dossier defines:

- Dominate migration envelope
- qualification rules
- legal language
- delivery gates
- Black Label escape hatches

## Working Strategic Conclusion

**Inference:** This is a credible evolution of Dominate, not a repudiation of ConversionOS.

The right direction is:

- shared core
- custom public shells where justified
- strong rights/domain qualification
- bounded premium delivery
- lighthouse validation through MD Construction before broad rollout
